Problem

Our focus is on solving the problem of navigating a camera along an array of monkey bars as an inital step towards surveilling a jungle environment. The goal is to create a semi-autonomous brachiating robot which can navigate between and along "branches" from one hold to another.

Concept

Our robot is designed to traverse monkey bars via a brachiating motion. Brachiation is completed through swinging from one bar to another bar with a non-actuated joint about the bar. The robot includes a stereo camera to identify the next bar's location in the sequence. An operator is in control over the robot remotely, having access to the robot's camera feed to make high level decisions on how the robot should proceed with the swing. Once the robot has identified the relative position of the next bar, it calculates whether it is reachable and the operator confirms. Once ready, the robot releases the rear gripper. The robot swings to generate the required energy to reach the target bar and then paths to grasp it. This sequence of operations can repeat based off the operator's inputs.
