Problem

Our focus is on solving the problem of navigating a camera along an array of monkey bars as an inital step towards surveilling a jungle environment. We propose a brachiating robot which will swing between and along the bars from one hold to another.

Concept

Our robot is designed to traverse across monkey bars via a brachiation motion. Brachiation is completed through swinging from one bar to another bar through only arms. The robot will include a stereo camera to identify the next bar in the sequence, with the closest desired direction bar being the next targeted bar. An operator will be in control over the robot via remote control, in addition to having access to the robot's camera feed to help decide how the robot should proceed with the swing. Once the robot has identified the relative position of the next bar, it will calculate whether it would be able to reach it. Once ready, the robot shall adjust to the starting position and release the gripper furthest from the direction of travel. As one arm falls, the motors should activate to reach the desired bar and grasp it. This sequence of operations will repeat based off the operator's inputs.
